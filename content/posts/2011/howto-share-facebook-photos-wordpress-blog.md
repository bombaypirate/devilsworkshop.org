Facebook has become the place of interest for internet users to hang around, play games, network, share photos and more. It&#8217;s quite a common trend that everyone uploads their pictures on Facebook. If you are willing to show those photos on your WordPress powered blog, this is the right tutorial for you. <img src="http://devilsworkshop.org/wp-includes/images/smilies/simple-smile.png" alt=":-)" class="wp-smiley" style="height: 1em; max-height: 1em;" />

### Get the Plugin

We will be using a plugin to implement the feature on the blog. <a href="http://www.aaronharp.com/dev/wp-fotobook/" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.aaronharp.com/dev/wp-fotobook/', 'Fotobook']);" >Fotobook</a> is a WordPress plugin that will link to your Facebook account and import all of your photo albums into a page on your WordPress installation. It makes use of Facebook&#8217;s API so importing your photos is a breeze.  
You can download the plugin <a href="http://downloads.wordpress.org/plugin/fotobook.3.2.2.zip" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article', 'http://downloads.wordpress.org/plugin/fotobook.3.2.2.zip', 'here']);" >here</a>

### Configure the plugin

Install the plugin from the admin section. and activate the plugin.

### **Step 1: Authenticate**

This step asks you to login to your Facebook account. This is the step by which you allow the plugin to access your account and fetch the images and publish to your blog. Clicking on the link opens a pop-up window as below. Enter your login credentials and click the login button.  
<a href="http://wpveda.com/howto-share-facebook-photos-wordpress-blog/3-2/" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article', 'http://wpveda.com/howto-share-facebook-photos-wordpress-blog/3-2/', '']);" rel="attachment wp-att-2441"><img class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-2441" src="http://cdn.devilsworkshop.org/files/2011/04/3.png" alt="" width="497" height="309" /></a>  
This will take you to the next step, that is grant access.  
<a href="http://wpveda.com/howto-share-facebook-photos-wordpress-blog/4-3/" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article', 'http://wpveda.com/howto-share-facebook-photos-wordpress-blog/4-3/', '']);" rel="attachment wp-att-2442"><img class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-2442" src="http://cdn.devilsworkshop.org/files/2011/04/4.png" alt="" width="495" height="314" /></a>  
After this step, you may close the pop-up window and proceed to the step 2.  
**Step 2 : Get Permissions**  
This step asks you to grant access for your photos and videos associated with your Facebook account.<a href="http://wpveda.com/howto-share-facebook-photos-wordpress-blog/5-3/" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article', 'http://wpveda.com/howto-share-facebook-photos-wordpress-blog/5-3/', '']);" rel="attachment wp-att-2443"><img class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-2443" src="http://cdn.devilsworkshop.org/files/2011/04/5.png" alt="" width="494" height="294" /></a>  
Once you allow the Fotobook plugin to do so, you can close the window and proceed to the next step.  
**Step 3: Apply Permissions**  
By clicking this, you apply all the steps done before and save the settings. Once you click Apply Permissions your Facebook account will be added and you can see something like below.  
<a href="http://wpveda.com/howto-share-facebook-photos-wordpress-blog/6-3/" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article', 'http://wpveda.com/howto-share-facebook-photos-wordpress-blog/6-3/', '']);" rel="attachment wp-att-2446"><img class="aligncenter size-large wp-image-2446" src="http://cdn.devilsworkshop.org/files/2011/04/6-520x261.png" alt="" width="520" height="261" /></a>

### Import Photos to blog

<a href="http://wpveda.com/howto-share-facebook-photos-wordpress-blog/6-4/" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article', 'http://wpveda.com/howto-share-facebook-photos-wordpress-blog/6-4/', '']);" rel="attachment wp-att-2447"><img class="aligncenter size-large wp-image-2447" src="http://cdn.devilsworkshop.org/files/2011/04/61-520x261.png" alt="" width="520" height="261" /></a>This will take you to the next step which is shown below.  
<a href="http://wpveda.com/howto-share-facebook-photos-wordpress-blog/7-4/" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article', 'http://wpveda.com/howto-share-facebook-photos-wordpress-blog/7-4/', '']);" rel="attachment wp-att-2448"><img class="aligncenter size-large wp-image-2448" src="http://cdn.devilsworkshop.org/files/2011/04/7-520x355.png" alt="" width="520" height="355" /></a>  
Clicking on **Get Albums** will fetch from your Facebook account and list all the albums related to your account. By this time, the plugin have created the exact replica of albums on your Facebook account. You can even hide the albums if you don&#8217;t want to show that specific album by clicking the hide link.  
<a href="http://wpveda.com/howto-share-facebook-photos-wordpress-blog/8-2/" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article', 'http://wpveda.com/howto-share-facebook-photos-wordpress-blog/8-2/', '']);" rel="attachment wp-att-2449"><img class="aligncenter size-large wp-image-2449" src="http://cdn.devilsworkshop.org/files/2011/04/8-520x394.png" alt="" width="520" height="394" /></a>

That&#8217;s it, you are done. You can now access the albums at *http://yourblogdomain/photos*  
The plugin also allows you to display photos in 3 styles.

  1. Color box.
  2. Embedded.
  3. Lightbox.

<a href="http://wpveda.com/howto-share-facebook-photos-wordpress-blog/9-2/" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article', 'http://wpveda.com/howto-share-facebook-photos-wordpress-blog/9-2/', '']);" rel="attachment wp-att-2450"><img class="aligncenter size-large wp-image-2450" src="http://cdn.devilsworkshop.org/files/2011/04/9-520x105.png" alt="" width="520" height="105" /></a>  
Select the style and you are all set. Do drop in your comments.
