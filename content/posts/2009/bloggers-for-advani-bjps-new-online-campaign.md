<a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bharatiya_Janata_Party" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article', 'http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bharatiya_Janata_Party', 'Bhartiya Janta Party']);" >Bhartiya Janta Party</a> (BJP) is gathering support of bloggers with new online campaign **“**<a href="http://www.lkadvani.in/eng/content/view/735/391/" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.lkadvani.in/eng/content/view/735/391/', 'Bloggers for Advani']);" ><strong>Bloggers for Advani</strong></a>**”**. They earlier [launched a blog and massive online advertising campaign][1] to create awareness about Prime Minister candidate.

In this new **“**<a href="http://www.lkadvani.in/eng/content/view/735/391/" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.lkadvani.in/eng/content/view/735/391/', 'Bloggers for Advani']);" ><strong>Bloggers for Advani</strong></a>**”** campaign, they have created round-shaped badges which bloggers can put on their blog to show their support for <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lal_Krishna_Advani" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article', 'http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lal_Krishna_Advani', 'Lal Krishna Advani']);" >Lal Krishna Advani</a>.

Considering large size of Indian blogosphere, BJP will surely gather considerable support from this new campaign.

### <span style="text-decoration: line-through">No link juice…</span>

<span style="text-decoration: line-through">One thing I have realized as a blogger is, if they (BJP) had put a listing like <em>“bloggers supporting us”</em> on their campaign page, more bloggers would have showcased these badges for extra link and traffic.</span>

<span style="text-decoration: line-through">In blogosphere, easiest way to attract links is giving backlinks in return!</span>
